Research perspective

Mathematics is most useful when it gives us a sharper way to see.

I study nonlinear models where small changes can organize large consequences: ecological interactions, epidemic spread, delayed feedback, and high-dimensional data. My work moves between theory, computation, and clear scientific communication.

03 / About

From a problem to a clearer world.

My work is built around a simple principle: theory should illuminate real systems, and computation should be designed with mathematical care.

I earned my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at the University of Toledo, following an M.A. in Applied Mathematics and a B.S. in Applied Mathematics from Lebanese American University. My doctoral work has centered on nonlinear dynamics, mathematical biology, and computational approaches to scientific questions.
