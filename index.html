01 / RESEARCH

Mathematical analysis for systems that change.

My work joins rigorous nonlinear analysis with computation to examine feedback, instability, delay, spatial structure, and data-informed dynamics.

Current themes
  • Bifurcation theory and nonlinear dynamicsHigh-codimension bifurcations, Bogdanov-Takens and nilpotent singularities, Hopf bifurcation, heteroclinic loops, and delay differential equations.
  • Mathematical biologyPredator-prey dynamics, epidemic models, density-dependent incidence, infection-dependent recovery, Allee effects, and reaction-diffusion systems.
  • Scientific computing and machine learningPhysics-informed neural networks, inverse problems for gene regulatory networks, feature maps, topological tools, and reproducible numerical workflows.

Doctoral research

My dissertation, High Codimension Bifurcations in Predator-Prey Models, develops a unified codimension-3 theory for nonlinear biological systems and analyzes mechanisms including Hopf bifurcations and saddle-node-to-saddle heteroclinic loops.
