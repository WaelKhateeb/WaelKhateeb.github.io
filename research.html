Doctoral research & projects

Ph.D. dissertation

High-codimension bifurcations in predator-prey models

Wael El Khateeb.

Ph.D. dissertation, University of Toledo.

Developed a unified codimension-3 framework for nonlinear biological systems, covering Bogdanov-Takens, nilpotent saddle, and Hopf bifurcations, together with criteria for detecting saddle-node-to-saddle heteroclinic loops and related high-codimension dynamics.

Research experience

Mathematically Justified Computational Platform for Nonlinear Dynamics

Machine Learning Research Assistant, University of Toledo; AFOSR-supported project under the supervision of William Kalies.

Developed neural-network approaches for phase-space representation and data-driven modeling; applied physics-informed neural networks to inverse problems for gene regulatory networks; implemented learnable feature maps to capture high-frequency patterns.

Undergraduate research mentorship

Florida alligator population modeling and policy analysis

Graduate Student Mentor, Williams College Polymath Junior Program.

Mentored a four-student research team studying Florida alligator population trends, guiding data analysis and the translation of modeling results into policy briefs and conference presentations.
